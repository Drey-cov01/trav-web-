CALABARZON REPRESENTS: QUEZON

Quezon, officially the Province of Quezon and historically known as Tayabas, is a province in the Philippines located in the Calabarzon region on Luzon. Lucena, a highly urbanized city governed separately from the province, serves as its the provincial capital and its most populous city.

Famous Tourist Attractions

Kamay ni Hesus

Kamay ni Hesus Shrine is a Roman Catholic healing church and popular pilgrimage site in Lucban, Quezon, under the Diocese of Lucena.

Highlights:

  • Healing Mass & Center
  • 50-Foot Christ the Redeemer Statue
  • Garden of Eden & Noah’s Ark

Villa Escudero Plantations and Resort

This serene resort on a coconut plantation by the Bulakin River offers bamboo villas, pools, rafting, an antiques museum, and a waterfall restaurant with dance shows.

Highlights:

  • Labasin Waterfalls Restaurant
  • Cultural Show
  • Carabao Cart Ride

Minor Basilica of St. Michael the Archange

The Minor Basilica of Saint Michael the Archangel, commonly known as Tayabas Basilica, is a Roman Catholic basilica located in Tayabas, Quezon, Philippines, under the Diocese of Lucena.

Highlights:

  • Baroque-style architecture with a distinct key-shaped layout.
  • Longest church nave in the Philippines, offering a grand interior.
  • Popular pilgrimage and heritage site in Tayabas, Quezon.

Saint Louis Bishop Parish Church

Lucban Church, officially known as Saint Louis Bishop Parish Church, is a Roman Catholic church in Lucban, Quezon, under the Diocese of Lucena.

Highlights:

  • Historical Catholic Church located in Lucban, Quezon.
  • Spanish-era architecture with intricate designs and stone walls.
  • Dedicated to St. Louis, Bishop of Toulouse, the town’s patron saint.

Alibijaban Island

Alibijaban is a small island in San Andres, Quezon, off the southeastern coast of Bondoc Peninsula, Luzon.

Highlights:

  • Pristine white sand beaches and crystal-clear waters.
  • Lush mangrove forests ideal for eco-tourism and nature lovers.
  • Great for snorkeling, camping, and island hopping.

Agos river

A river in the Quezon province, formed by the confluence of the Pagsanjan River and the Umiray River. It flows into the Philippine Sea and is known for its role in irrigation and local ecosystems.

Highlights:

  • Rich biodiversity, surrounded by lush forests and wildlife.
  • Scenic and unspoiled river flowing through Quezon and Rizal provinces.
  • Major water source that supports local communities.

Bulakin River

The Bulakin River is a 7-kilometer river flowing through Villa Escudero Plantations and Resort, located in Tiaong and Dolores, Quezon, Philippines. It is known for its bamboo rafting activities, offering visitors a peaceful way to enjoy nature.

Highlights:

  • Peaceful and unspoiled nature spot in Quezon Province.
  • Ideal for swimming, picnics, and nature retreats.
  • Crystal-clear waters surrounded by lush greenery.

Balagbag falls

Balagbag Falls is a scenic waterfall located in Real, Quezon, Philippines. It is known for its twin cascades, clear waters, and natural pools, making it a popular destination for nature lovers and adventure seekers.

Highlights:

  • Beautiful multi-tiered waterfalls with natural pools.
  • Easily accessible in Real, Quezon.
  • Great for swimming, trekking, and picnics.

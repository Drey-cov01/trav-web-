Discover the Historical Charm of the Minor Basilica of St. Michael the Archangel

Explore the Minor Basilica of St. Michael the Archangel, one of the oldest and grandest churches in the Philippines, located in Tayabas, Quezon. This centuries-old church is a remarkable example of Baroque architecture and a testament to the town's rich religious heritage.

Minor Basilica of St. Michael the Archangel

Key Features and Attractions:

  • Baroque Architecture: Admire the intricate designs and stunning facade.
  • Bell Tower: One of the tallest in the region, offering panoramic views.
  • Religious Artifacts: Discover ancient religious items inside the basilica.

Activities and Entertainment:

  • Historical Tours: Learn about the church's architectural features, religious significance, and its role in local history.
  • Pilgrimage Tours: Participate in spiritual journeys and attend masses held regularly at the basilica.
  • Photography: Capture stunning views of the basilica's intricate facade and bell tower.

Delight in local flavors, just steps from the Basilica

Enjoy a variety of local and delicious dining options near the Minor Basilica of St. Michael the Archangel, offering a taste of tradition and comfort to complement your visit.

Why Visit "Minor Basilica "

  • Religious and Historical Significance The basilica is a prominent Catholic church with deep historical roots, serving as a place of devotion and pilgrimage for centuries.
  • Architectural Beauty Its stunning Spanish colonial architecture, intricate stained glass windows, and grand altar make it a visual masterpiece.
  • Cultural Heritage The basilica is a recognized cultural landmark, reflecting the rich heritage and religious traditions of the local community.
  • Peaceful Atmosphere The serene ambiance inside the church provides a perfect spot for prayer, reflection, or simply escaping the hustle and bustle of daily life.

Operating Hours & Fees:

  • the basilica offers daily Mass at 6:00 AM, with additional services on Sundays at 8:00 AM, 9:30 AM, 3:00 PM, 5:00 PM, and 7:00 PM.

Fun Fact:

The Minor Basilica of St. Michael the Archangel is the longest Spanish colonial church in the Philippines, measuring around 103 meters (338 feet) from the main door to the altar — earning it the nickname “Susi ng Tayabas” (Key of Tayabas) because its floor plan resembles a key!

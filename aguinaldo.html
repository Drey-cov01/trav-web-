Relax & Explore the Historical Significance of Aguinaldo Shrine

Discover the rich history, cultural importance, and architectural beauty of the Aguinaldo Shrine, located in Kawit, Cavite, Philippines. This national shrine is the ancestral home of Emilio Aguinaldo, the first president of the Philippines, and the site where the country's independence from Spain was declared on June 12, 1898.

Unveil the Beauty of Aguinaldo Shrine

Aguinaldo Shrine is a National Shrine located in Kawit, Cavite, and is the site where the Philippine Declaration of Independence was proclaimed on June 12, 1898. The house was originally built in 1845 and reconstructed in 1849 using limestone and hardwood.

Key Features and Attractions:

  • Historical Significance: The site of the Philippine Declaration of Independence and the first hoisting of the Philippine flag.
  • Architectural Beauty: Features a blend of late 19th-century architecture with motifs of the Philippine flag and other national flags.
  • Hidden Passages: Includes secret rooms and hidden compartments used during the revolution.
  • Independence Balcony: The iconic balcony from which the independence was proclaimed.

Activities and Entertainment:

  • Historical Exploration: Engage in guided tours to learn about Aguinaldo's life and the Philippine Revolution.
  • Cultural Walks: Wander through the shrine grounds and explore its historical significance.
  • Photography: Capture stunning views of the shrine and its surroundings.
  • Cultural Events: Attend lectures and performances celebrating Filipino culture.

Aguinaldo Shrine: Historic Dining Nearby

While exploring the Aguinaldo Shrine, visitors can find nearby restaurants offering hearty Filipino meals. Tapsilog, bulalo, and kare-kare are just some of the dishes you can try at eateries close to this historic site. Enjoy local delicacies while reflecting on the important history tied to this area.

Why Visit Aguinaldo Shrine?

  • Historical Significance: Offers insights into the Philippines' struggle for independence.
  • Cultural Experience: Provides a glimpse into traditional Filipino architecture and culture.
  • Accessibility: Located in Kawit, Cavite, making it a convenient day trip from Manila.

Operating Hours & Fees:

  • Shrine Hours: Open daily from 8 AM to 4 PM2.
  • Entrance Fees: Generally free, though donations are appreciated.
  • Guided Tours: Available upon request.

Fun Fact:

Aguinaldo Shrine was declared a National Shrine in 1964 and is managed by the National Historical Commission of the Philippines. It is a significant cultural and historical landmark, marking the beginning of the Philippines' journey as an independent nation.

CALABARZON REPRESENTZ: LAGUNA

Laguna is a province in the CALABARZON region of Luzon, Philippines. Known for its natural hot springs, waterfalls, and scenic lakes, Laguna is a top destination for nature lovers and adventure seekers. The province is home to the famous Pagsanjan Falls, the serene Lake Pandin, and the historic town of Pila with its Spanish-era architecture. Laguna also boasts the Enchanted Kingdom, the Philippines' premier amusement park, and Mount Makiling, a popular spot for hiking and eco-tourism. The dry season from December to May is the best time to visit, offering pleasant weather for outdoor activities. The province's local cuisine features Buko Pie (Coconut Pie) and Espasol, must-try delicacies for visitors. The primary language spoken is Tagalog, but English is widely used, especially in business and tourism sectors. The Philippine Peso (PHP) is the official currency, with many establishments accepting cash, credit cards, and mobile payment systems like GCash and PayMaya.

Famous Tourist Attractions

Reconstructed ancestral home of Dr. José Rizal, showcasing Spanish-Colonial architecture and memorabilia.

Bahay ni Rizal

Highlights:

  • A reconstructed ancestral home where Dr. José Rizal was born.
  • Showcases Rizal's personal belongings, books, and literary works.
  • Features a garden with a statue of young José Rizal, symbolizing his passion for knowledge.

The Philippines' first and largest theme park, known for its thrilling rides and entertainment.

Enchanted Kingdom

Highlights:

  • The Philippines' first and largest theme park located in Santa Rosa, Laguna.
  • Offers thrilling rides, family-friendly attractions, and nightly fireworks shows.
  • Features the iconic Eldar the Wizard mascot and themed zones like Agila the EKsperience.

Known for its scenic boat ride called "shooting the rapids," offering an adventurous river journey.

Pagsanjan Falls

Highlights:

  • One of the most famous waterfalls in the Philippines, located in Pagsanjan, Laguna.
  • Accessible through an exciting canoe ride along the Pagsanjan River.
  • Surrounded by lush greenery and towering rock formations, perfect for nature lovers.

Dormant volcano and popular hiking destination, famous for its biodiversity and scenic trails.

Mt.Makiling

Highlights:

  • A legendary dormant volcano known for its mystical beauty and lush forests in Laguna.
  • Popular for hiking trails leading to scenic views and rich biodiversity.
  • Home to rare plant species and a protected area for wildlife conservation.

Less crowded and ideal for those who love quiet nature trips.

Bunga Twin Falls (Nagcarlan)

Highlights:

  • A hidden gem in Nagcarlan, Laguna, featuring twin cascading waterfalls.
  • Surrounded by lush greenery and natural rock formations, perfect for relaxation.
  • Ideal for hiking, swimming, and nature photography.

A stunning waterfall surrounded by lush greenery, offering a refreshing escape into nature.

Taytay Falls

Highlights:

  • A picturesque waterfall in Majayjay, Laguna, known for its crystal-clear waters.
  • Nestled in a lush forest, offering a serene and refreshing ambiance.
  • Popular for hiking, swimming, and nature adventures.

One of the tallest waterfalls in Laguna, reachable through a scenic trek that also leads to nearby Talay and Hidden Falls.

Hulugan Falls

Highlights:

  • A majestic waterfall in Luisiana, Laguna, with a towering cascade of cool waters.
  • Surrounded by verdant forests and scenic landscapes, perfect for nature lovers.
  • Popular for trekking, swimming, and photography.

Offers serene waters ideal for bamboo rafting, swimming, and enjoying traditional Filipino meals by the lake.

lake pandin

Highlights:

  • A serene freshwater lake in San Pablo, Laguna, known for its emerald-green waters.
  • Surrounded by lush forests and scenic landscapes, offering a peaceful nature retreat.
  • Popular for bamboo rafting, swimming, and enjoying fresh local delicacies.

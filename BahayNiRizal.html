"Preserving a National Treasure"

The Bahay ni Rizal (Rizal Shrine) is the ancestral home of Dr. José Rizal, the national hero of the Philippines. It is located in Calamba, Laguna, where Rizal was born on June 19, 1861. The house is a reconstructed replica of the original Spanish colonial-style home, which was destroyed during World War II. The current structure was rebuilt in 1950 under the supervision of the Philippine government.

Key Features:

  • Architecture: The house is built in the traditional "Bahay na Bato" style, featuring stone on the lower floor and hardwood on the upper floor.
  • Exhibits: Inside, you’ll find Rizal’s memorabilia, including family photographs, books, and replicas of his writings.
  • Library and Study Room: Reflects Rizal's love for learning and intellectual pursuits.
  • Kitchen and Dining Area: Shows how Filipino families lived during the Spanish colonial era.
  • Garden and Courtyard: The outdoor area gives visitors a glimpse of the environment where young Rizal spent his childhood.

Significance:

The Bahay ni Rizal serves as a museum that honors Rizal's life, works, and legacy. It’s a popular destination for students and tourists who want to learn about Rizal's upbringing and the influences that shaped his thoughts and contributions to Philippine independence.

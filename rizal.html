CALABARZON REPRESENTS: RIZAL

Rizal is a province in the CALABARZON region of Luzon, Philippines, named after José Rizal, a national hero. It has historical significance because of its proximity to Metro Manila and as part of the larger capital region, which influences the country's development and culture. Rizal is endowed with natural beauty, including the Sierra Madre mountain range and historical sites such as the Baras Church, Antipolo Cathedral, and Casa Santa. The province has a mix of urban and rural regions with a significant cultural impact. The best time to visit Rizal is during the dry season, which runs from December to May and is perfect for outdoor activities such as trekking and visiting parks. The colder months of December through February are ideal for sightseeing, while the rainy season of June through November can bring severe rains. The predominant language spoken in Rizal is Tagalog, the Philippines' national language. In cities, English is generally spoken, but rural people speak local dialects and Tagalog variants. The Philippine Peso (PHP) is the official currency of Rizal, and most cities and municipalities have ATMs. Credit and debit cards are accepted at malls, restaurants, and hotels in urban areas.

Famous Tourist Attractions

Pinto Art Museum (Antipolo)

The Pinto Art Museum in Antipolo, Rizal, is a contemporary art museum that showcases local and international artists' works in a vast estate with gardens and traditional Filipino architecture.

Highlights:

  • Extensive collection of contemporary Filipino art.
  • Beautifully landscaped gardens surrounding the museum.
  • Panoramic views of Antipolo and the surrounding areas.

Daranak Falls (Tanay)

Daranak Falls in Tanay, Rizal is a popular natural attraction with a stunning cascade into a blue pool, offering picnic spots, trails, and a serene escape from urban life for nature lovers and adventure seekers.

Highlights:

  • The beautiful cascade of Daranak Falls.
  • Swimming and picnicking in the natural pool below the falls.
  • A peaceful, nature-filled environment.

Masungi Georeserve (Tanay)

Masungi Georeserve in Tanay, Rizal is an eco-tourism destination known for its limestone formations, forests, and scenic trails. It offers eco-adventures like trail walks, rope courses, and the "Sapot," making it ideal for nature lovers and adventure enthusiasts.

Highlights:

  • Conservation efforts to protect the area's unique ecosystem.
  • Spectacular views of the Sierra Madre mountain range.
  • Scenic trails and eco-adventures, including hanging bridges and rope courses.

Luljetta's Hanging Gardens and Spa (Antipolo)

Luljetta's Hanging Gardens and Spa in Antipolo offers a luxurious nature retreat with stunning views of the valley and Manila Bay, featuring outdoor pools, gardens, spas, and a unique "Hanging Gardens" for relaxation and rejuvenation.

Highlights:

  • The Hanging Gardens with scenic views of Antipolo.
  • Outdoor spa and wellness treatments.
  • Swimming pools with serene, natural backdrops

Sierra Madre Mountain Range (Tanay)

The Sierra Madre Mountain Range, the Philippines' longest mountain range, is known for its diverse wildlife, stunning landscapes, hiking trails, and excellent opportunities for nature photography and eco-tourism.

Highlights:

  • Breathtaking mountain views and hiking trails.
  • Rich biodiversity, including native flora and fauna.
  • Peaceful nature retreats and camping spots.

Mt. Pamitinan (Rodriguez)

Mount Pamitinan, a popular hiking destination in Rodriguez, Rizal, is renowned for its stunning rock formations and stunning views of the Sierra Madre mountain range and Marikina Valley, and is part of the Pamitinan Protected Landscape.

Highlights:

  • Stunning views of the Sierra Madre mountain range and Marikina Valley.
  • Unique rock formations, including massive boulders and cliffs.
  • A relatively challenging hike with rewarding views

Pililla Windmill (Pililla)

Pililla Windmill Farm, located in Pililla, Rizal, is a renewable energy site with towering wind turbines offering sweeping views of Laguna de Bay and the Sierra Madre mountains. It is an eco-tourism spot perfect for those looking to explore clean energy in a scenic environment.

Highlights:

  • Stunning panoramic views of Laguna de Bay and the Sierra Madre mountain range.
  • Iconic wind turbines, making it a unique and environmentally conscious landmark.
  • A peaceful and educational stop for visitors interested in renewable energy and nature.

Morong Beach (Morong)

Morong Beach, located in Bataan but often associated with nearby Rizal, is a quiet coastal destination known for its clean sand, crystal-clear waters, and peaceful environment. It’s a perfect spot for a relaxing getaway, offering a mix of relaxation and nature exploration.

Highlights:

  • Calm, clear waters perfect for swimming and sunbathing.
  • Secluded location, offering a less crowded beach experience.
  • Beautiful sunset views, ideal for a tranquil end to the day.

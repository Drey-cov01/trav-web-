CALABARZON REPRESENTS: BATANGAS

Batangas is a province in the CALABARZON region of Luzon, Philippines. It is known for its stunning natural landscapes, historical significance, and thriving tourism industry. One of its most famous landmarks is Taal Volcano, one of the smallest yet most active volcanoes in the world, located within Taal Lake. Batangas also played a crucial role in Philippine history, being home to many national heroes and revolutionaries during the country’s struggle for independence. The province is dotted with well-preserved heritage houses and churches, particularly in Taal Heritage Town, showcasing Spanish-era architecture.The best time to visit Batangas is during the dry season, from December to May, when the weather is ideal for beach outings, hiking, and outdoor activities. The rainy season, from June to November, can bring heavy rainfall, which may affect outdoor excursions.The primary language spoken in Batangas is Tagalog, which is also the official language of the Philippines. English is widely spoken, especially in business and tourism areas. The currency used in Batangas, as in the rest of the country, is the Philippine Peso (PHP). Most establishments accept cash, and ATMs are available in cities and major towns.

Famous Tourist Attractions

Laiya, San Juan

Laiya in San Juan, Batangas, is a pristine beach destination known for its fine white sand and crystal-clear waters. It is a favorite spot for weekend getaways, offering resorts, water sports, and a relaxing atmosphere perfect for families and friends.

Highlights:

  • White sandy beaches and crystal-clear waters
  • Ideal for swimming, snorkeling, and beach camping
  • Resorts and beachfront accommodations for a relaxing stay

Masasa Beach, Tingloy

Masasa Beach, located in Tingloy, Batangas, is a hidden gem with its unspoiled beauty and serene surroundings. The beach boasts soft sand and vibrant marine life, making it ideal for snorkeling and diving.

Highlights:

  • Stunning white sand beach with turquoise waters
  • Excellent snorkeling and diving spots with rich marine life
  • Peaceful and less crowded, perfect for nature lovers

Caleruega Church

Caleruega Church, nestled in Nasugbu, Batangas, is a peaceful retreat center known for its scenic landscapes and beautiful chapel. It is a popular venue for weddings and spiritual retreats, offering a serene atmosphere for reflection.

Highlights:

  • Picturesque hilltop church with breathtaking views
  • Popular venue for weddings and spiritual retreats
  • Beautiful gardens and peaceful surroundings for meditation

Verde Island

Verde Island, situated between Batangas and Mindoro, is considered one of the most biodiverse marine ecosystems in the world. It is a must-visit for divers, featuring colorful coral reefs and a vast array of marine species..

Highlights:

  • Renowned diving destination with vibrant coral reefs
  • Pristine beaches and clear waters for snorkeling
  • Serene island getaway with a relaxing natural ambiance

Fortune Island

Fortune Island, located in Nasugbu, Batangas, is famous for its Greek-inspired ruins overlooking turquoise waters. Visitors can explore the island’s dramatic cliffs, dive into its clear blue sea, and experience breathtaking sunset views..

Highlights:

  • Greek-inspired ruins offering scenic photography spots
  • Cliff diving and snorkeling in crystal-clear waters
  • Stunning views of the West Philippine SeaGreat for hiking enthusiasts

Fantasy World

Fantasy World, located in Lemery, Batangas, is an abandoned theme park that resembles a medieval castle. It has become a popular spot for photography and sightseeing, with its fairytale-like structures and lush surroundings.

Highlights:

  • Fairytale-inspired castle and theme park perfect for photography
  • Scenic views of Batangas countryside from the towers
  • A unique destination for family and group trips

Monte Maria Shrine

Monte Maria is not just a shrine but an international pilgrimage and conference center built in honor of the Virgin Mary. Formally known as Montemaria International Pilgrimage and Conference Center, the complex spans 8 hectares and is situated 20 kilometers outside of Batangas City.

Highlights:

  • Home to the towering statue of the Virgin Mary
  • Offers panoramic views of Batangas Bay and the South China Sea
  • A pilgrimage site with a peaceful atmosphere for prayer and reflection

Taal Basilica

Taal Basilica’s official name is Basilica of St. Martin de Tours. It stands on top of a hill at the center of Taal Town. This basilica is huge; it is 96 meters high and 45 meters wide. It is often referred to as the largest Catholic Church in Asia.

Highlights:

  • The largest Catholic church in Asia, rich in history and architecture
  • Overlooking Taal’s heritage town with Spanish-era houses
  • A cultural landmark showcasing intricate baroque-style interiors

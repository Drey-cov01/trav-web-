Relax & Explore the Innovation of Pililla Wind Farm

Discover the stunning landscapes, cutting-edge technology, and exciting activities of Pililla Wind Farm, located in Barangay Halayhayin, Pililla, Rizal, Philippines. This wind farm is a testament to the Philippines' commitment to renewable energy and offers a unique blend of nature and innovation.

Unveil the Beauty of Pililla Wind Farm

Pililla Wind Farm is a 54 MW wind energy project featuring 27 wind turbines, each standing at 125 meters tall. It is part of a natural wind corridor, making it an ideal location for harnessing wind power.

Key Features and Attractions:

  • Wind Turbines: Offers a spectacular view of rotating blades against the clear blue sky.
  • Laguna de Bay Views: Provides breathtaking views of the lake and surrounding hills.
  • Renewable Energy: A significant source of clean energy, contributing to the Philippines' sustainable development goals.
  • Visitor Center: Educates visitors about the benefits of wind power and its impact on the environment.

Activities and Entertainment:

  • Photography: Capture stunning shots of the wind turbines and surrounding landscapes.
  • Exploration: Wander through nearby trails and explore the natural beauty of the area.
  • Event Hosting: Use the wind farm as a backdrop for intimate events or educational gatherings.
  • Educational Tours: Learn about renewable energy and its role in sustainability.

Why Visit Pililla Wind Farm?

  • Innovative Technology: Offers a unique opportunity to witness renewable energy in action.
  • Natural Beauty: Provides stunning views of Laguna de Bay and the surrounding hills.
  • Accessibility: Located just two hours from Manila, making it a convenient day trip destination.

Operating Hours & Fees:

  • Farm Access: Open daily; specific hours may vary.
  • Entrance Fees: Minimal fees apply; guided tours are available upon request.
  • Transportation: Accessible via public or private transport from Manila.

Fun Fact:

Pililla Wind Farm is not only a significant source of renewable energy but also a popular tourist destination, attracting over 130,000 visitors during peak seasons like Holy Week. It has received the IFC Sustainable Energy Finance Award for its innovative financing structure and commitment to sustainable development.

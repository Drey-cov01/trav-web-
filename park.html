Relax & Explore the History of People’s Park in the Sky

Discover the breathtaking views, rich history, and unique charm of People’s Park in the Sky, located in Tagaytay City, Cavite, Philippines. Originally built as a mansion during the Marcos era, this historic urban park offers visitors panoramic vistas of Taal Lake, Taal Volcano, and the surrounding Tagaytay Highlands.

Unveil the Beauty of People’s Park in the Sky

People’s Park in the Sky was originally named "Palace in the Sky" and built during Ferdinand Marcos’ regime on Mount Sungay (also known as Mount Gonzales), Tagaytay’s highest point. Construction began in 1981 but was halted after Reagan canceled his visit. In 1986, following Marcos’ ouster, it was converted into a public park symbolizing resilience and transformation.

Key Features and Attractions:

  • Palace Ruins: Explore remnants of the unfinished mansion built for Reagan’s visit.
  • Shrine of Our Lady: A religious site featuring an image of the Virgin Mary and Child Jesus.
  • Doppler Radar Station: Operated by PAGASA to monitor weather patterns.
  • Panoramic Views: Offers stunning vistas of Taal Lake, Taal Volcano, and nearby provinces.

Activities and Entertainment:

  • Exploration: Walk through historical structures while enjoying cool mountain air.
  • Photography: Capture scenic landscapes and architectural highlights.
  • Event Hosting: Use the park for special celebrations or cultural events.

People’s Park in the Sky: A Scenic Meal Above the City

At People’s Park in the Sky, visitors can enjoy snacks from local vendors offering turon (banana spring rolls), bibingka (rice cake), and sago’t gulaman (sweet tapioca drink). The relaxed and scenic atmosphere makes it a great spot to enjoy a simple meal or snack while overlooking the beautiful Tagaytay landscape.

Why Visit People’s Park in the Sky?

  • Historical Significance: Offers insights into Philippine history during the Marcos era.
  • Natural Beauty: Combines historical structures with breathtaking views of nature.
  • Accessibility: Located just two hours from Manila, making it an ideal day trip destination.

Operating Hours & Fees:

  • Park Hours: Open daily from 8 AM to 5 PM.
  • Entrance Fees: Approximately ₱30 per person; additional fees may apply for parking or activities.

Fun Fact:

People’s Park in the Sky sits atop Mount Sungay at 2,326 feet above sea level. It was initially seen as a symbol of extravagance during Marcos’ rule but has since been transformed into a public space celebrating resilience and hope. Its location provides one of the best vantage points for viewing Tagaytay’s iconic landscapes.
